About Platypus

The platypus is a unique mammal found only in Australia. It is a semi-aquatic animal that spends most of its time in the water.

It has a bill like a duck, a tail like a beaver, and webbed feet. Males have venomous spurs on their hind legs, making them one of the few venomous mammals.

Habitat

Platypuses are found in freshwater streams and rivers in eastern Australia, from the tropical rainforests of Queensland to the cool highlands of Tasmania.

They build burrows in the banks of rivers and streams, where they rest and rear their young.

Behavior

Platypuses are mainly active at night, and feed on insects, crustaceans, and small fish.

They use electroreception to detect prey in the water, and can close their eyes, ears, and nostrils while diving to hunt underwater.
